Overview

Purpose

My goal is to develop my company and acquire more customers. I require this website in order to help people and increase my client base and my potential market. First, I want to expand my business locally and start marketing my website to my genuine clientele. By doing this, I will have a strong base of customers in my state. Second, I need to start contacting potential clients outside of my state in order to build my business. My business will gain from having a wider audience and more customers.

Audience

My ideal clients are those who are curious about how to fix and maintain basic issues with their PCs. If they encounter more complicated issues, they can find my contact information and bring their PC to me. My website will focus on customers who fall into the 16 to 23 age range and have the means to purchase a PC. They want to learn quick fixes for PC issues or quick tricks to speed up their computer; if they have more complex issues, they will bring the computer to me. Advanced troubleshooting, individualized customer service, and hardware tips are needs that are not being met.

Content

Home page

People can see the website name and a banner at the top of this page. As they scroll down, they will find a grid with 8 tips and tricks in the form of cards, detailing how to fix minor issues with their PCs and speed up their computers. Some of the questions they might have include:

  • Why does my computer take so long to start?
  • I don't have a lot of files, so why does my computer have no space?
  • How do I clean out my computer's temporary files and trash?
  • how do I optimize Windows for my outdated computer?
  • how to install the most recent version of windows?
  • How can I check the specs on my computer?
  • How do I update my computer drivers?

    • My website will provide quick and easy tutorials with steps to answer each question in order to answer those questions. My clients will choose this site over others because the instructions are clear and the content is delivered quickly. In order to finish these tutorials, I will detail how to do so in my own words, step by step. If images are required, I will attach them, but the main goal is to avoid doing so so that the steps are clear and easy to read.

    Images for the Home page

    FAQ

    As users scroll down, they will see 6 frequently asked questions about their computers or operating systems, along with a headline and a brief description at the top. There are no tutorials on this page; only answers to frequently asked questions that don't need any specific instructions. Some of the questions they might have:

  • When should I update my computer?
  • How can I tell if my computer is old?
  • How can I tell if my computer will meet my needs?
  • Which is superior, a laptop or a PC?
  • Can you help me fix my computer?
  • Can I bring my computer to you?

    • My clients can find answers to the most frequently asked questions on this FAQ page of the website. There is no need for a tutorial for any of the questions, and they are not listed on the home page. My clients will visit this page to find out more information and get answers to some of their questions that the home page is unable to provide. Additionally, they will learn about the PC repair services I offer and whether they can visit me in person or contact me virtually for help.

    Images for the FAQ page

    About Us

    Users will see a picture of my store and a few pictures of a repairman (not my actual photos; I'll use stock internet photos), along with 2 questions and one paragraph of text for each question, as they scroll down. The text will be brief. They can learn about my business, where I am based, where my store is, how long I've been working with computers, and details about my education and PC repair training. They will visit this page to confirm the legitimacy of my business, discover more about me, and find out where to bring their PC for support. Some of the questions that they may have before opening this page are:

  • Is this business legit?
  • Why should I believe this company's PC advice?
  • Where can I find out more about this industry?
  • Where do I go to get my computer fixed?
  • What's the business address to take my PC?
